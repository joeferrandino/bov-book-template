Property Description

Property Description

75 South Highland Avenue is a two-story, multi-tenant professional/medical office building of approximately 3,950 square feet, built in the late 1970s with a brick-and-anodized-aluminum exterior. The building occupies a corner position at South Highland Avenue (Route 9) and Maurice Avenue, with surface parking for approximately 14 vehicles.

The property is 100% leased across four suites ranging from 925 to 1,075 SF. Tenants pay their own electric and are responsible for replacing their own A/C units (one recently replaced). The owner pays heat (oil), hallway and exterior electric, and exterior repairs; the town provides trash collection. Recent capital includes new gutters; minor masonry pointing is the only noted near-term item.

The rent roll is anchored by quality, established tenants on staggered leases running through 2028, each carrying 3% annual escalations. With tenants covering their own electric and A/C and full occupancy consistently maintained, the asset produces a stable, well-managed, and largely hands-off income stream.

Investment Highlights
  • Stable In-Place Income — 100% leased with contractual rent in place across four suites; a dependable, market-rate income stream.
  • 100% Occupied, Quality Tenants — Staggered leases 2026–2028; two 5-year renewal options to 2032; no near-term rollover risk.
  • Strong, Hands-Off Management — Full occupancy maintained; tenants pay their own electric and fund A/C, limiting owner expense exposure.
  • Built-In Escalations — 3% annual increases across all leases steadily grow income.
  • Modest Upside & Location — Below-market Suite 2-1 resets at its 2027 rollover; ~19,000 VPD Route 9 frontage near Metro-North.

Location Summary
Market & Location Overview

South End Plaza sits on South Main Street in Thomaston, in the Naugatuck Valley. The property is less than a mile from Route 8 (Exits 38 and 39), the expressway that runs the length of the valley. Route 8 connects south to Waterbury in 10 to 15 minutes and on toward Bridgeport and the shoreline, and north toward Torrington. U.S. Route 6 runs through Thomaston's Main Street, east to Bristol and southwest to Watertown.

Thomaston is a historic mill town named for the clockmaker Seth Thomas, whose factory helped bring the Naugatuck Railroad through the valley. Its walkable downtown centers on the 1884 Thomaston Opera House, which still serves as the town hall and home to the Landmark Community Theatre. The Railroad Museum of New England runs heritage excursions from the historic Thomaston Station. The town keeps a working manufacturing base in metalworking, electronics, defense, and packaging.

The borough is reinvesting in its center, with Sustainable CT certification, Naugatuck River Greenway construction, Seth Thomas Park renovations, and a stated focus on housing and economic development. Thomaston is not itself a Metro-North stop. The nearest station is the Waterbury Branch terminus about nine miles south, which connects to the New Haven Line toward New York.

Recommendation

We recommend marketing the property at $865,000, representing a 6.7% cap rate on in-place income. This pricing should attract strong investor interest by offering both an attractive going-in yield and a compelling pro forma return, with contractual rent increases already built into the leases driving the cap rate toward approximately 8.0% over the hold period.

Given current market conditions and the higher interest rate environment, we believe the property is most likely to trade near $815,000, or roughly a 7.1% cap rate on in-place income. That said, the asset benefits from several characteristics that could support pricing above that level, including a stable roster of local tenants, built-in rent growth, and strong in-place cash flow. As a result, while a sale around $815,000 represents a realistic underwriting assumption, there is a credible path to achieving a higher sale price through a competitive marketing process.

Current Lending Environment

Typical commercial real estate interest rates today fall in the high-6% to low-7% range. At an expected $815,000 purchase price, a buyer would likely invest approximately $253,670 of equity and finance the remaining $561,330.

The investment is supported by the property’s in-place income, not future projections. Based on the in-place NOI of $58,174, the property generates sufficient cash flow to cover estimated annual debt service of approximately $46,540, resulting in a 1.25x debt coverage ratio and an estimated 4.8% cash-on-cash return in place, rising to approximately 9.4% on a pro forma basis. Many investors target cash-on-cash returns in the 8% to 12% range for stabilized assets, a level the property reaches as in-place rents mark to market.
