The Asset
Property Description

75 South Highland Avenue is a two-story, multi-tenant professional/medical office building of approximately 3,950 square feet, built in the late 1970s with a brick-and-anodized-aluminum exterior. The building occupies a corner position at South Highland Avenue (Route 9) and Maurice Avenue, with surface parking for approximately 14 vehicles.

The property is 100% leased across four suites ranging from 925 to 1,075 SF. Tenants pay their own electric and are responsible for replacing their own A/C units (one recently replaced). The owner pays heat (oil), hallway and exterior electric, and exterior repairs; the town provides trash collection. Recent capital includes new gutters; minor masonry pointing is the only noted near-term item.

On trailing-twelve actual net operating income of $65,393, the $815,000 value reflects an 8.02% capitalization rate — squarely in line with what the market pays for stable, fully-leased office product of this type. Income is supported by quality, established tenants on staggered leases through 2028, each carrying 3% annual escalations, producing a stable, largely hands-off stream.

Investment Highlights
  • Stable In-Place Income — $65,393 trailing-twelve NOI; an 8.02% cap at the asking price, market-rate for this product type.
  • 100% Occupied, Quality Tenants — Staggered leases 2026–2028; two 5-year renewal options to 2032; no near-term rollover risk.
  • Strong, Hands-Off Management — Full occupancy maintained; tenants pay their own electric and fund A/C, limiting owner expense exposure.
  • Built-In Escalations — 3% annual increases across all leases steadily grow income.
  • Modest Upside & Location — Below-market Suite 2-1 resets at its 2027 rollover; ~19,000 VPD Route 9 frontage near Metro-North.

Location Summary

Ossining is an affluent Hudson River village in Westchester County, roughly 30 miles north of New York City. The village is served by the Metro-North Hudson Line, with express service reaching Grand Central in under 50 minutes — anchoring a strong commuter and professional base.

South Highland Avenue (Route 9) is the village's primary north-south commercial corridor. The subject benefits from a high-visibility corner with an estimated traffic count exceeding 19,000 vehicles per day, supporting consistent professional and medical-office demand.

The surrounding trade area pairs affluent household incomes with a stable, established population base — a combination that underpins durable demand for professional and medical services in the immediate corridor.

Market & Disposition Considerations
Target Buyer Profile

At a sub-$1M price point, 75 South Highland is well-suited to the private investor market — local owner-operators, 1031 exchange buyers seeking a stabilized replacement asset, and medical/professional users. The accessible entry point keeps financing broadly available and deepens the buyer pool.

  • 1031 Exchange Buyers — Stabilized, fully-leased income at a sub-$1M basis; ideal replacement property.
  • Local Private Investors — Westchester / Hudson Valley familiarity; opportunity to self-manage.
  • Medical / Professional Users — Steady small-suite demand from healthcare and service tenants.
Cap Rate Market Commentary

Stable, fully-leased office assets of this size in Westchester trade at roughly an 8.00% cap rate. Ownership has done an excellent job keeping the building 100% occupied with quality tenants, making this a stable, largely hands-off investment well-suited to a private buyer or 1031 exchange. At $815,000 the asset is priced at an 8.02% cap on trailing-twelve in-place income — squarely market for this product — corroborated by the confirmed 7.90% cap at 1250 Pleasantville. The conservatively underwritten 7.14% current cap and 8.53% pro forma cap provide additional downside protection and upside.

Positioning Strategy

NPCG recommends presenting 75 South Highland as a stabilized, income-producing professional office asset with mark-to-market upside. The narrative should emphasize (1) 100% occupancy with staggered terms, (2) contractual escalations and renewal options, and (3) a high-income Westchester trade area with strong Metro-North access.

  • Broker Network Outreach — Direct targeting of Westchester, Hudson Valley, and CT investment-sales brokers.
