Property Description
1504 Route 9W · Marlboro, NY 12542
Property Description

Marlboro Commons is a fully leased neighborhood retail center consisting of approximately 11,426 square feet on a 2.0-acre site. The property features eight storefront retail spaces facing Route 9W, along with a 1,500-square-foot warehouse space leased to Bottini Fuel. The center offers ample on-site parking and has served local businesses for decades.

The property is currently 100% occupied. All tenants operate under a similar gross lease structure, with the landlord responsible for real estate taxes, insurance, maintenance, and property operating expenses, while tenants pay their own utilities, interior maintenance, and business-related expenses. Suite 2 was recently leased to a new tenant beginning in April 2026.

Every lease includes scheduled rent increases over the next several years, providing built-in income growth through 2028. The property is served by private well and septic systems, with reserves included in the underwriting for ongoing maintenance and future capital needs.

Lease Structure & Tenancy
  • Gross Leases: Landlord pays heat, water/sewer, trash, grounds, taxes, insurance, CAM.
  • No Tenant Reimbursement: No CAM, tax, or insurance pass-through.
  • Contractual Escalations: Fixed and 5% step-ups across all spaces, 2026–2028 (per owner).
  • Diversified Tenancy: Local service & retail mix.

Location Summary
Market & Location Overview

The property is located on Route 9W in Marlboro, a commercial corridor serving residents and businesses throughout the western Hudson Valley. Route 9W connects Newburgh to the south and Kingston to the north, with access to Poughkeepsie via nearby Hudson River crossings. Traffic counts on this section of Route 9W are approximately 16,500 vehicles per day.

Marlboro is supported by a growing residential population and household incomes that are above state and national averages. The Town of Marlborough has a population of roughly 9,400 residents, while median household income is approximately $107,000 townwide and about $119,000 within the 12542 ZIP code.

The area supports a mix of local retailers, service businesses, and community-oriented uses that serve the surrounding residential base. The subject property is a fully leased retail center with direct Route 9W frontage, providing tenants visibility and convenient access within one of Marlboro’s established commercial areas.

Appraisal Reconciliation

A 2023 certified appraisal by NY Appraisals Inc. concluded a value of $1,430,000. The appraisal relied on a lower expense load and a more aggressive cap rate than the market supports today.

Reconciling to Today’s Value

Less: Cap Rate to Market (7.25% to 9.0%)($240,000)

1 · Rising Cap Rates

Cap rates have risen broadly across retail since the 2023 valuation, a function of higher lending rates and a return to market reality after the low-rate era. The appraisal’s 7.25% reflects that prior environment; today’s cost of capital prices the asset meaningfully higher. Re-pricing the income to current rates is the single largest adjustment.

2 · Expenses Understated

The $1,430,000 rests on $103,700 of NOI against $154,434 of stabilized gross income, only a 33% expense and vacancy load. On the subject’s gross-lease structure the sustainable load is 40% (2025 actuals ran 48%), trimming stabilized NOI to roughly $90,000.

Recommendation

We recommend marketing the property at $1,125,000, representing an 8.0% cap rate on current in-place income. This pricing should attract strong investor interest by offering both an attractive going-in yield and a compelling pro forma return, with contractual rent increases already built into the leases driving the cap rate to approximately 9.73% over the hold period.

Given current market conditions and the higher interest rate environment, we believe the property is most likely to trade near $1,000,000, or roughly a 9.0% cap rate on current income. That said, Marlboro Commons benefits from several characteristics that could support pricing above that level, including 100% occupancy, a stable roster of local tenants, built-in rent growth, and strong in-place cash flow. As a result, while a sale around $1,000,000 represents a realistic underwriting assumption, there is a credible path to achieving a higher sale price through a competitive marketing process.

Current Lending Environment

Typical commercial real estate interest rates today fall in the high-6% to low-7% range. At an expected $1,000,000 purchase price, a buyer would likely invest approximately $300,000 of equity and finance the remaining $700,000.

The investment is supported by the property’s current income, not future projections. Based on the current normalized NOI of $89,913, the property generates sufficient cash flow to cover estimated annual debt service of approximately $54,500, resulting in a 1.65x debt coverage ratio and an estimated 11.8% cash-on-cash return. For context, many investors target cash-on-cash returns in the 8% to 12% range for stabilized retail assets, placing this opportunity near the upper end of that spectrum.
